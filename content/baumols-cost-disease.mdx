Wage growth in industries with low or limited productivity growth — is now called **Baumol’s Cost Disease**. 

It has been used to explain the increase in the cost of a variety of in-person services and as an argument for why some services — particularly healthcare, education, and the arts — should be funded by the state and not by for-profit entities.
